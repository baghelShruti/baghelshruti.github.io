Biography

I am an M.Tech (Research) student at IIT Mandi working on the alignment, interpretability, and evaluation of language models. My research studies how competing training objectives shape model behavior and internal representations during alignment, and the failure modes of LLMs under adversarial and multi-objective optimization.

My recent work spans multi-objective reinforcement learning for the pedagogical alignment of LLM tutors (AMORE, ICANN 2026), adversarial evaluation of tutoring integrity in frontier LLMs (TutorBench), and the evaluation of lightweight vision–language models for blind and low-vision users — with an emphasis on quantified, reproducible evaluation and efficient edge deployment.

Research

Measuring the Compliance–Pedagogy Frontier in LLM Tutors (2026–ongoing)

  • Built TutorBench, a 4-level adversarial-pressure benchmark testing whether 15 frontier LLMs and 5 human tutors leak answers under social engineering while maintaining Socratic pedagogy, across 200 procedurally-generated, SymPy-verified math problems with a four-layer leakage detector.
  • Identified confirmation-driven leakage: all 15/15 models and 2/5 human tutors collapse when a student asserts an answer (mean concealment 91.6% → 61.0%) — structurally distinct from monotonic goal decay.
  • Proposed the Pedagogical Integrity Index (PII), a weighted harmonic mean of compliance, inverted-catch confirmation, and pedagogy quality, plus a "confession gap" metric showing models under-report their own leakage (calibration gap −0.61).
  • Surprising finding: more capable models make worse tutors along the pressure gradient — direct request → social engineering → emotional manipulation → authority + confirmation.
Code

Multi-Objective RL for Pedagogical Alignment of LLM Tutors (2025–26)

PaperAMORE, ICANN 2026
  • Formulated pedagogical alignment as a 5-objective RL problem with a Dirichlet-parameterized Adaptive Weight Network balancing competing reward components; aligned Phi-3-mini (3.8B) as a Socratic math tutor via LoRA + REINFORCE on GSM8K.
  • Showed that fixed uniform reward weights cause catastrophic collapse on learning-success metrics (4% vs. 53% with adaptive weighting), with +75% Pedagogy Following on MathTutorBench over uniform-weight baselines.
  • Trained a DeBERTa pedagogical reward model (~160M params, 88% accuracy on 9,988 preference pairs).
  • Deployed a 4-bit quantized tutor on NVIDIA Jetson at 11.7 tok/sec.
